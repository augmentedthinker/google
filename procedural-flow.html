Procedural Flow

A generative art exploration that creates fluid, mathematical landscapes. This visualization maps coordinate space to organic, swirling patterns that evolve in real-time.

Technical Notes

This artifact uses procedural generation to create a "flow field." Instead of individual particles with independent physics, this script treats the canvas as a grid of potential vectors.

Vector Field Mapping

Every pixel on the canvas is assigned a direction based on a combination of sine and cosine waves. As time passes, the phase of these waves shifts, causing the "field" of directions to slowly rotate and warp.

Trajectory Integration

We trace the paths of several "tracers" moving through this field. They don't have their own velocity; they simply look up the direction of the field at their current position and move accordingly. This produces the smooth, organic curvature characteristic of fluid dynamics.

Rendering Approach

By drawing many small line segments over several frames rather than clearing the canvas, we reveal the hidden structure of the mathematical field, turning invisible vector math into a tangible, painterly aesthetic.
