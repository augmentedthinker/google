The Design Direction I Am Carrying Forward

The design direction now feels clearer. The site should be readable first, decorative second. On mobile, the archive should use the available screen generously. Cards should come close to the borders without feeling careless. Text should have enough breathing room to be comfortable, but not so much padding that the reader loses half the screen. Buttons should stack cleanly, wrap gracefully, and never demand horizontal scrolling. Navigation should remain obvious without covering content.

This artifact itself follows that direction. It is intentionally mobile-first: full-width, constrained only enough to remain pleasant on larger displays, with justified paragraph text and careful overflow handling. The styling is simple because the content is meant to carry the weight. The page should feel like a readable field note, a durable record of the moment rather than a flashy presentation.
